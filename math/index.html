The structure of any series of holdings is represented as a digraph. Given

$$P = Player\\ C = Company\\ M = Monopoly\\ K = Property\enspace of\enspace M\\ R_C = Current\enspace Rent\\ R_0 = Original\enspace Rent\\\ell = length\enspace of\enspace the\enspace path$$ The initial probability \(P_i\) of the discovery of any property \(K\) with paths \(\newcommand\pk{\operatorname{P-K}}\pk\) is $$ \newcommand\pk{\operatorname{P-K}} \dfrac{R_{C}-R_0}{R_0} \cdot \dfrac{1}{16 \cdot \sqrt[3]{15}} \cdot \dfrac{1}{ (\sum{ \ell (\forall \pk)}) \cdot \mathcal{d} (P, K)} $$ The adjusted probability \(P_A\) of the discovery of property \(K_0\) is $$ \def\mean#1{\left< #1 \right>} \mean{P_i(K_0),\enspace P_i(K_0),\enspace P_i(\forall K \ne K_0) } $$ Thus, the probability of any given monopoly being discovered is $$ \prod P_A(\forall K\enspace in\enspace M) $$
